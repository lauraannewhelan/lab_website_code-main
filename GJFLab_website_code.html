ABOUT OUR WORK

Deciphering the causes of genetic eye diseases & designing innovative therapies

Visual Impairment

Inherited retinal degenerations (IRDs) are the most frequent cause of vision loss in people of working age, while age-related macular degeneration (AMD) and glaucoma are increasingly prevalent eye diseases. All greatly impact quality of life and health economics.

Genetics

The Trinity team characterized some of the first IRD genes globally and is now at the forefront of genetic profiling of IRDs at a population scale using next generation sequencing (NGS). This research has, for the first time, resulted in defining the genetic architecture of these disorders in Ireland.

Gene Therapy

A key goal is to develop novel interventions to treat blindness. The team has investigated the molecular causes of IRDs and has explored novel therapies addressing critical problems such as the immense genetic heterogeneity inherent in IRDs with innovative technologies.

Outreach

The team is actively involved in outreach and dissemination of science to a variety of audiences. We share our work via press, TV, radio, podcasts, social media, workshops as well as public and patient involvement, participation and engagement events, among others.
